## Best Oreo Protein Ice Cream

**Base type:** Lite Ice Cream

This recipe aims to create an ice cream that tastes like real cookies and cream while having the macros to eat it every day, featuring a unique technique for soft Oreo chunks.

### Ingredients

- 1 whole Oreo
- 410g (about 1⅔ cups) fat-free high protein milk
- 4.2g (1 tsp) vanilla extract
- 1 scoop whey protein (preferably cookies and cream flavor)
- 10g (about 1 tbsp) erythritol
- 4g (about ¾ tbsp) black cocoa powder
- 0.5g (scant ⅛ tsp) salt
- 0.5g (about ⅙ tsp) xanthan gum
- 2 whole Oreos (for mix-ins)
- 118g (4 fl oz / ½ cup) milk (for dunking mix-in Oreos)
- 1 whole Oreo (for topping)

### Steps

1.  Grab a high-sided container and place it on a scale.
2.  Add 1 whole Oreo to the container.
3.  Pour 410g (about 1⅔ cups) fat-free high protein milk and 4.2g (1 tsp) vanilla extract over the Oreo.
4.  Add 1 scoop whey protein, 10g (about 1 tbsp) erythritol, 4g (about ¾ tbsp) black cocoa powder, 0.5g (scant ⅛ tsp) salt, and 0.5g (about ⅙ tsp) xanthan gum to the container.
5.  Mix the dry ingredients thoroughly within the container.
6.  Use an immersion blender to start mixing the wet ingredients.
7.  Slowly start adding the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients by allowing a small amount to drop in, then waiting until it dissolves before adding a little bit more. Do this over a 30-second period.
8.  Continue mixing the liquid base for an additional 90 seconds until smooth and combined.
9.  Pour the finished liquid base into a Ninja CREAMi pint container, put a lid on it, and place it level in the freezer for a full 24 hours.
10. To prepare the mix-ins, get about 118g (4 fl oz / ½ cup) milk in a glass. Dunk each of the 2 whole Oreos into the milk for 15 to 20 seconds. Place the dunked Oreos in a plastic bag and throw them in the freezer with your Creami pints to freeze.
11. After 24 hours, remove the frozen pint from the freezer and rinse it under hot water for one minute.
12. Process the pint on the "Sorbet" setting.
13. Once the sorbet spin finishes, take a knife and make a hole near the edge of the pint.
14. Grab the 2 pre-dunked and frozen Oreos from the freezer, cut them in half, and fill the hole in the pint with these Oreos.
15. Cover the Oreos with the ice cream that was removed to create the hole.
16. Load the pint back into the Ninja CREAMi and hit the "Mix-in" setting.
17. While the Oreos are getting mixed in, chop up 1 more Oreo for a topping.
18. Pull the finished ice cream out, top it with the chopped Oreo pieces, and enjoy.

### Notes

-   Almond milk will make this ice cream more icy and will have less of an ice cream mouthfeel while significantly reducing protein, so it is not advised unless you don't drink milk.
-   If you have calories to spare, you can use 2% or even whole milk to get a top-tier cookies and cream experience.
-   If you prefer a gelato-like consistency, use a whey casein cookies and cream protein powder instead of just whey protein.
-   The quality of the protein powder's flavor is crucial; use high-quality protein with a good flavor profile.
-   Black cocoa powder is used because it is exactly what Oreo uses in their cookies, giving them their dark color.
-   When adding dry ingredients, slowly sprinkle them in while continuously mixing to prevent clumping.
-   The "Sorbet" setting was found to be the best for an ice cream-like consistency for this specific recipe, with the "Lite Ice Cream" setting being a close second.
-   The special mix-in technique of dunking Oreos in milk and freezing them separately ensures soft, chunky Oreos throughout the ice cream.
-   When preparing the dunked Oreos for mix-ins, it's suggested to do six to eight Oreos at a time if you plan to make this ice cream regularly.
-   It is important not to take the dunked and frozen Oreos out of the freezer until it is time to add them as mix-ins, as they come back to room temperature very quickly.
-   Topping with a chopped dry Oreo provides a perfect contrast of crunchy Oreos on top with soft chunks throughout the cream.
